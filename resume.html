Food Deserts

As part of a hackathon to investigate food insecurity in the United States, I took some time to take a look at the locations of grocery stores within the United States. Using GIS data from Open Street Map, I plotted the locations of full service grocery stores within a selection of five major U.S. cities. We then defined a food desert as any place where a there is no full service grocery store within ~ 0.8 miles (as the crow flies), or about a 15-20 minute walk for the average person. This is a slightly shorter radius than what the USDA defines as a food desert, but considering how quickly some perishable foodstuff can go bad if not refrigerated, and the challenge of travel and transporting grocers for the eldery and those with disabilities, a more realistic distance was required.

What I found was interesting, if not somewhat horrifying. Large sections of some cities like Houston have massive areas within city limits without a grocery store in the area. Other cities such as Chicago see more nearly two thirds of thier grocery located where more than 95% of their trade area is also served by another grocer store in the area, a clear example of Hotelling's Law in action.

SQL for Data Viz

To help aspiring data visualization professionals, I wanted to create an open source course to introduce the basics of SQL to those unfamiliar. The target audience are younger professionals who know how to visualize data in tools like Tableau and Power BI, but may be intimidated or hesitatant to learn SQL. The course walks each participant through installing the software and importing data before preparing their first SQL query. The course skips past the bits like stored procedures that would fall on a database administrator, and by the end, participants are joining tables, creating CTEs, writing sub-queires, and writing their own statements to bring the data they need into their tool of choice.

And the best part, the course doesn't use another sample dataset with fake customers and sales. It uses real data from the NFL so anyone can make their own fantasy football dashboard.

Fantasy Football Draft Board

After years of failing to find success in my Fantasy Football league, it was time to make a change in how I drafted players every year. Using data from the NFL, I wanted to see who the top performers were the pervious year, and graphed the total points scored in 2021. However it's not just about points scored, consistency is important too! By measuring how consistent a player performed, I could draft to avoid players who either boom or bust, and have confidence that I'll have a winning chance every week. Additionally included is the 2022 Average Draft Position (ADP) to highlight the consensus thinking, and a method to track players who have already been drafted, who is on a watchlist, and positions that have already been filled.

Income Tax Calculator

As a tool to learn Python, I developed a script that would ask the user a few basic questions such as their marital status, taxable income, and the year they were filing, to calculate their income tax liability. The script would pull from a basic table on GitHub that contained the various tax brackets and the associated rates.

Once all the inputs have been collected, the python script will calculate the income tax liability using the progressive tax brackets for the appropriate year and filing status.

This tool should not be considered tax advice. Consult your attorney or a Certified Public Accountant for questions.
